1. Monkey 47

Schwarzwald Dry Gin | 47% ABV

Black Forest, Germany

Flavor Profile

Monkey 47 delivers an extraordinary complexity with a distinctly woody, earthy profile balanced by bright citrus and floral notes. The initial taste overwhelms with spice, moving to citrus in the middle, and finishing with earthy sweetness. Its nose offers an intricate blend of lavender, chamomile, lemon verbena, cardamom, and mint, with deeper layers of ginger and lemongrass.

Key Botanicals (47 in total, including):

Juniper Lingonberries Lavender Chamomile Spruce Shoots Cardamom Lemon Verbena Ginger Lemongrass

Distillation Process

Crafted at the Black Forest Distillery by master distiller Christoph Keller, Monkey 47 is made using a traditional method with a molasses-based spirit. The 47 botanicals, many foraged from the Black Forest, are macerated in the base spirit before being distilled in small batches through traditional copper pot stills. The gin is then matured in earthenware containers for three months before being brought to bottle strength with local spring water.

4. Tanqueray

London Dry Gin | 47.3% ABV

Cameron Bridge, Scotland

Flavor Profile

Tanqueray is celebrated for its perfectly balanced, crisp flavor with dominant juniper notes complemented by subtle citrus and spice. The palate begins with juniper and finishes with rich hints of baking spice including angelica root, cinnamon, and coriander seed. Tanqueray's approach to gin-making is famously minimalist, focusing on quality over quantity of botanicals to create a spirit that's bold yet incredibly smooth.

Key Botanicals:

Juniper Coriander Angelica Root Licorice

Distillation Process

Tanqueray employs a one-shot distillation method using a neutral grain spirit as its base. The botanicals are steeped in the base spirit for 24 hours before distillation in their famous No. 4 copper pot still, nicknamed "Old Tom." This still has a unique design with a distinctive pineapple-shaped top section that helps refine the flavors during distillation. The gin is distilled four times to achieve exceptional purity and smoothness, with the precise recipe dating back to Charles Tanqueray's original formula from 1830.

The distillery employs a vapor basket infusion for some of the more delicate botanicals, while others are steeped directly in the pot. The base spirit is made from Australian wheat, and the gin is cut with water from the Yarra Valley, known for its purity. Four Pillars also embraces a sustainability ethos, using the spent botanicals and oranges from distillation to make marmalade, further reducing waste. This attention to detail and focus on Australian ingredients has helped establish Four Pillars as one of the world's most awarded gin distilleries.

Distillation Process

Oxley employs a truly revolutionary production method that sets it apart from all other gins—cold distillation under vacuum. While most gins are distilled at temperatures around 80°C (176°F), Oxley's patented process occurs at -5°C (23°F), achieving distillation through pressure reduction rather than heat. This "sub-zero distillation" took over eight years to perfect and requires a custom-built, sophisticated still. The botanicals are macerated in the base spirit at room temperature for about 15 hours before being placed in the cold distillation chamber. This process preserves the fresh, volatile flavors that would normally be altered or lost with heat distillation. The result is a gin that captures the true essence of each botanical with exceptional clarity and brightness. The production is also notably small-batch, with each run producing only 240 bottles, allowing for meticulous quality control.
